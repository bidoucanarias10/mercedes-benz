You can not stop progress. This is the first thought that comes to mind after trying the new 510hp AMG C63 S Coupé V8 biturbo. Up to now offered with 7 speeds, it has 9 reports with the 2019 vintage. It will not be marketed until October but Sport-Auto.ch has already had the privilege of trying it. Take the twin-turbo V8 from the Mercedes-AMG GT coupe tested last year and graft it into a C Series, then you'll have an explosive cocktail offered in sedans, station wagons, convertibles and coupes. Although each of these bodies was present during the press tests organized in Germany, I chose the Coupé. This is the sportiest version and it is with such a bodywork that Mercedes-AMG is aligned in the DTM championship, in particular with the Geneva driver Edoardo Mortara.

As soon as I leave Padeborn Airport, north of Frankfurt, I prefer a matte gray-black coupe. The exact name of its color is “designo graphite gray magno”. Before I get behind the wheel, take a look under the hood. Unlike in mass production, AMG engines are assembled by hand. From fitting the crankshaft and camshafts to the various connections, each propellant is fitted by a single technician who signs his signature on the AMG engine plate. Such a label was also found on the engine of the Nissan GT-R tested last summer. This was also assembled by hand.
